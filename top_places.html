Top 10 places you can visit in Kolkata

Kolkata, the state capital of West Bengal, is renowned for its rich past. This is clear through the cuisine, buildings, cultures, and people there. It can be overwhelming to try to take it all in in one city, but fortunately, we've put together a list of the things Kolkata is best known for. Have a look!It is one the most sought after destinations for the tourists from all over the globe. The British Raj-era architectural gems, sprawling gardens and historical colleges, universities, with museums, National Library, Auditoriums, Theater Halls, Art galleries, markets, The Festivals, sports stadium, Gangar ghats are of great attractions. It has many places of great pilgrimage. Known as the cultural capital of India, Kolkata continues to spawn generations of poets, writers, film producers and Nobel Prize winners. If your trip only allows for a visit of one or two of Indias metropolitan cities, than definitely consider placing Kolkata on your itinerary. You definitely wont forget the city on the Hooghly and a boat/ launch trip on it is truly enjoyable.

1 . VICTORIA MEMORIAL


The Victoria Memorial houses a museum with a vast collection of historical artifacts and artworks from the British Raj period. Visitors can explore galleries dedicated to paintings, sculptures, manuscripts, and other exhibits that offer insights into India's rich cultural heritage.The Victoria Memorial is surrounded by beautiful gardens that are spread over 64 acres of land. The gardens are meticulously maintained and offer visitors a peaceful and scenic place to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.In front of the memorial, there is a statue of Queen Victoria, which is a popular spot for taking photographs.The Royal Gallery inside the museum is one of the most popular sections of the museum. It contains a large collection of portraits and memorabilia related to the British royal family, including rare photographs, letters, and personal items.

2.HOWRAH BRIDGE

Howrah Bridge in Kolkata is a popular tourist attraction for its unique cantilever design, stunning views of the city and iconic status as one of India's busiest bridges. It is also an important symbol of the city and a major landmark that has been featured in many films and songs. Visitors to Kolkata can take a boat ride underneath the bridge or simply admire it from afar. The bridge is illuminated at night, making it even more spectacular.

KALIGHAT

3.Kalighat in Kolkata is a famous pilgrimage site for Hindus, as it is home to one of the 51 Shakti Peethas - sites where parts of the body of the Hindu goddess Sati are said to have fallen. The temple here is dedicated to Goddess Kali and draws thousands of devotees from all over India each year. Kalighat is also known for its vibrant art scene, with numerous galleries showcasing traditional and contemporary works by local artists.

4.SCIENCE CITY

Science City in Kolkata is a scientific and educational center that attracts both children and adults alike. Opened in 1997, it is the largest science centre in India and features a number of interactive exhibits, including a 3D theatre, an energy park, and a virtual reality zone. It also hosts regular events such as science shows, workshops, and seminars. Science City is a great place to learn about the world around us through hands-on activities and displays.

5.INDIAN MUESEUM

The Indian Museum in Kolkata is the oldest and largest museum in India, established in 1814. The museum houses a wide range of artifacts including coins, sculptures, paintings, textiles, fossils, and mummies from around the world. It also has a library containing rare manuscripts and books on natural history and anthropology. Visitors can explore the various galleries which display different aspects of human culture from ancient to modern times. The Indian Museum is an important educational center for students and researchers alike, providing a unique opportunity to understand the cultural heritage of India.

6. ST. PAUL'S CATHEDRAL

St. Paul's Cathedral in Kolkata is one of the city's most famous churches, built in 1847 and modeled after St. Martin-in-the-Fields in London. The cathedral is renowned for its Gothic architecture, stained glass windows, and intricate stone carvings. It is also home to a number of important artifacts, including a painting of the Last Supper by Italian artist Angelo Varni. Visitors can attend services at the church or simply marvel at its beauty from outside.

9. MAIDAN


Maidan has a long and fascinating history. Originally, it was a salt water lake, which was drained in the 18th century to make way for a military parade ground. Over time, it became a popular recreational area for the people of Kolkata, and many sports clubs and cultural organizations were established in the area. Maidan is a popular venue for sports and physical activities. It is home to several sports clubs, including the famous Mohun Bagan Athletic Club and the Calcutta Cricket and Football Club. The park has facilities for a wide range of sports, including cricket, football, hockey, and tennis. Despite being located in the heart of the city, Maidan is a green oasis that is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The park has several water bodies, including the famous Rabindra Sarobar Lake, which attracts migratory birds in the winter months.

10. NATIONAL LIBARY


The National Library of India was established in 1836 as the Calcutta Public Library. It was renamed the National Library of India in 1948, and was designated as the country's national library in 1953. The library has a rich history and has been an important repository of knowledge for over 180 years. The National Library of India has a vast collection of books, manuscripts, and other materials. It is the largest library in India, with over 2.2 million books, including rare and valuable manuscripts, maps, and other documents. The library has a special focus on Indian history, culture, and literature, and also has a large collection of foreign publications.The National Library of India provides a wide range of services to its users. It has reading rooms where visitors can access books and other materials, as well as internet and computer facilities. The library also provides services such as inter-library loan, document delivery, and reference services.The National Library of India hosts many cultural events throughout the year, including book launches, exhibitions, and lectures. The library also hosts the annual Kolkata Book Fair, which is one of the largest book fairs in Asia.
